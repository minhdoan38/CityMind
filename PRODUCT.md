# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Primary: local officers and ops staff who review citizen incident reports, check AI triage, update status, and clear high-priority work with clear evidence and confidence.

Secondary: residents who report neighborhood problems (text, location, optional photo) and later check status without creating an account.

Context is bilingual EN/VI civic use — often on mobile for citizens, desktop for officers.

## Product Purpose

CityMind AI turns fragmented citizen reports into structured, prioritized, auditable insights so officers can verify evidence and decide faster. Citizens get a simple way to submit issues and track progress. AI is advisory decision support only — never autonomous incident response.

Success looks like officers clearing high-priority reports faster with clear evidence, confidence, and an auditable trail.

## Positioning

AI-assisted decision intelligence for smart communities: structure and prioritize citizen reports; officers remain the final authority.

## Brand Personality

Clear, trustworthy, civic. Voice is plain and direct — like a well-run city service, not a startup pitch. Calm confidence over hype; bilingual copy should feel equally natural in English and Vietnamese.

## Anti-references

- Generic purple SaaS / AI-startup landing pages
- Dark cyber / neon “smart city” dashboards
- Dense bureaucratic government portals (avoid clutter and jargon walls even while staying civic)

## Design Principles

1. **Civic clarity first** — plain language, obvious next actions, no AI theater.
2. **Officers decide; AI advises** — UI must never imply autonomous authority or prediction.
3. **Equal weight, one default** — dashboard is the product default; public report flows stay simple and 311-like with clear CTAs.
4. **Trust through evidence** — show status, history, confidence, and uncertainty honestly.
5. **Bilingual by design** — EN/VI are first-class, not an afterthought.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA as the bar. Prefer status cues that don’t rely on color alone; respect reduced motion where animations exist.
